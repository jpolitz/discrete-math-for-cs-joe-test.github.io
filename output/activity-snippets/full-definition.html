When \(A\) and \(B\) are sets, \(A = B\) (set equality) means \[\forall x ( x\in A \leftrightarrow x \in B)\] When \(A\) and \(B\) are sets, \(A \subseteq B\) (“\(A\) is a subset of \(B\)") means \[\forall x (x \in A \to x \in B)\] When \(A\) and \(B\) are sets, \(A \subsetneq B\) (“\(A\) is a proper subset of \(B\)") means \[(A\subseteq B) \wedge (A \neq B)\] Definition: A function \(f: D \to C\) is onto (or surjective) means for every \(b\) in the codomain, there is an element \(a\) in the domain with \(f(a) = b\). Formally, \(f: D \to C\) is onto means \(\underline{\phantom{\forall b \in C \exists a \in D ( f(a) = b)}}\).

A relation \(R\) on a set \(A\) is called symmetric means \((b, a) \in R\) whenever \((a, b) \in R\), for all \(a, b \in A\).

A relation \(R\) on a set \(A\) is called transitive means whenever \((a, b) \in R\) and \((b, c) \in R\), then \((a, c) \in R\), for all \(a, b, c \in A\).
